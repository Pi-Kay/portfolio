Music AI Compliance Navigator

Interactive tool helping artists, labels, and music tech companies navigate EU AI Act, GDPR, and artist rights obligations

Problem

AI compliance in music is fragmented across legal documents, technical specs, and evolving regulations. Independent artists lack resources to navigate it. Companies risk building unethical pipelines without realizing it.

Current resources are either too legal (inaccessible) or too simplified (incomplete).

Solution

A decision-tree tool that works bidirectionally: artists can check if their work was used without consent, and companies can verify their AI pipeline is ethical. Input a use case, get requirements, risks, and action steps.

Use Cases (v1.0)

01
Consent & Attribution
Was this data/voice/work used with permission? What attribution is required?
Artists checking exploitation · Companies verifying pipeline
02
Training Data Provenance
Where did this model's training data come from? How to document and verify?
Artists auditing AI tools · Companies proving compliance
03
Generated Output Rights
Who owns what I made with AI? Can I sell it? What do I owe?
Musicians releasing AI-assisted work · Labels clearing rights
04
Transparency & Disclosure
When must I say "AI was used"? To whom? How?
Artists building trust · Companies meeting EU AI Act

These use cases are bidirectional—useful whether you're an artist protecting yourself or a company trying to do right.

Context

This project sits at the intersection of AI ethics and creative technology—part of a broader inquiry into how perceptual systems (human and artificial) interact, and what obligations emerge when AI mediates sensory experience.

Future directions: accessibility in generative media, neuroaesthetic considerations in immersive AI, sensory consent frameworks.

Structure designed for transferability: medical AI consent, engineering traceability, accessibility voice banking, creative tech broadly.

Process

AI-Human Collaboration

This project documents AI collaboration transparently. Human decisions drive strategy, scope, and ethical framing. AI assists with research synthesis, code drafting, and structural suggestions.
