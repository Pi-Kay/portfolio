Context

Valispace builds software for aerospace and systems engineers — managing requirements, specifications, and traceability across complex hardware-software projects. 100K+ prototypes on the platform.

I joined as solo UX designer. The challenge: fragmented workflows, unclear requirements, engineers who couldn't articulate what they needed because the problems were embedded in how they worked.

Role

UX Architect for Systems Engineering

Embedded research and design — remote and on-site. Working directly with aerospace engineers, product managers, and software developers in Bremen (Germany) and Lisbon (Portugal).

Approach

I started from how engineers actually work, not from interface patterns or feature requests.

  • Embedded collaboration — sat with engineers, observed workflows, asked "show me how you do this"
  • Think-aloud testing — prototypes tested in real work context, not lab settings
  • Interviews — surfaced unspoken constraints: regulatory requirements, verification logic, traceability dependencies
  • Iterative prototyping — Figma prototypes evolved through continuous feedback

Impact

  • Secured €100K+ development contract through interactive prototypes
  • Founded cross-functional UX Guild — bridging design, engineering, and domain expertise
  • Drafted design principles adopted by the team
  • Advocated for and achieved design team expansion
  • Core features survived acquisition — still in production today

What I Learned

Complex B2B systems require embedded research — you can't design for engineers from a distance.

The real requirements are unspoken, buried in workflow friction and regulatory constraints. Prototypes are conversations, not deliverables.
